## Process Overview
The current loan application and risk review process at Union Bank involves multiple manual steps and handoffs across Relationship Management, Credit, Risk, Compliance, and Operations teams. The process relies on manual data entry, spreadsheet-based financial analysis, and email-driven communication, resulting in longer turnaround times and limited visibility into application status.

---

## AS-IS Process Flow

### 1. Loan Application Submission
- Customer submits loan application and supporting documents to the Relationship Manager (RM)
- Submissions occur via branch visits or email

### 2. Initial Review for Completeness
- RM manually reviews application and documents
- Missing information requires follow-up with the customer

### 3. Loan Application Entry
- RM manually enters customer and loan data into the Loan Application Registry (LAR)
- Supporting documents are uploaded manually

### 4. Credit & Financial Analysis
- Credit Analysts review financial statements
- Risk metrics and ratios are calculated using Excel spreadsheets

### 5. Risk & Underwriting Review
- Underwriters assess risk exposure and credit analysis
- Decisions are communicated via email

### 6. Compliance Review
- Compliance team validates regulatory and policy requirements
- Issues are escalated manually

### 7. Final Decision & Customer Notification
- Approved or rejected decisions are sent to the RM
- RM notifies customer via phone or email

### 8. Loan Booking & Disbursement
- Operations team books the loan in the core banking system
- Funds are disbursed to the customer

---

## Current-State Pain Points
- Manual data entry across multiple systems  
- Spreadsheet-based risk calculations  
- Email-driven approvals and escalations  
- Limited process transparency  
- Rework due to incomplete documentation  

---

## Business Impact
- Increased loan processing turnaround time  
- Higher operational and compliance risk  
- Reduced customer satisfaction  
- Inefficient use of resources
